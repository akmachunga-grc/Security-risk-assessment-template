A professional, field-ready information security risk assessment template designed specifically for **oil and gas small and medium enterprises (SMEs)** operating in Nigeria and the West Africa region.

Developed by a security professional with 11 years of experience spanning military service, critical infrastructure protection, and offshore oil and gas operations.

---

## Why This Template Exists

Most publicly available risk assessment templates are generic. They do not account for:

- The threat landscape specific to Nigerian and West African oil and gas operations
- SCADA and OT/ICS exposure in field and offshore environments
- Third-party and contractor access risks common to O&G SMEs
- Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023 compliance obligations
- Business Email Compromise patterns documented across the Nigerian energy sector

This template addresses all of the above in a single, structured document.

## What Is Included

- **Document Control** — version tracking, ownership, and classification fields
- **Purpose and Scope** — assessment boundary definition guidance
- **Risk Assessment Methodology** — likelihood and impact scales, color-coded risk matrix
- **Asset Identification Register** — categorized asset inventory with criticality ratings
- **Threat Catalogue** — 10 threats mapped specifically to the O&G SME context
- **Risk Register** — 5 completed sample entries for a fictional O&G SME, plus blank rows
- **Risk Treatment Plan** — treatment options (mitigate, transfer, avoid, accept) with residual risk tracking
- **Statement of Applicability** — ISO 27001:2022 Annex A control mapping
- **Review and Approval Records** — sign-off framework for governance documentation
- **Appendices** — revision history, glossary, and references

## How To Use This Template

1. Download the `.docx` file
2. Complete the Document Control section (Section 1)
3. Define your assessment scope (Section 2)
4. Populate the Asset Identification Register (Section 4) with your organization's actual assets
5. Map applicable threats from the catalogue (Section 5) to your identified assets
6. Complete the Risk Register (Section 6) — calculate scores using the matrix in Section 3
7. Develop your Risk Treatment Plan (Section 7)
8. Review ISO 27001 Annex A applicability (Section 8)
9. Obtain management sign-off (Section 9)
